## 4. Company History

- 1866
  - **Founded** as Colombo Ice Company by **Arthur Von Possner**; initial capital of **₤1,600**; staff of 22; 2 steam engines.
- 1880
  - Acquired the medical hall of **J Maitland**.
- 1883
  - Introduced Aerated Water with the distinctive **“Elephant”** trademark, evolving into the **Elephant House** brand.
- 1894
  - Merged with competitor Tom Walker’s company to form **New Colombo Ice Company Limited**; first MD **Tom Walker**.
- 1921
  - Introduced crown corks for aerated water.
- 1925
  - Began building a cold storage facility for frozen products.
- 1932
  - New Colombo Ice Company Limited acquired **Ceylon Creameries Limited**; started reconstituted milk and ice cream production.
- 1934
  - Purchased **Ceylon Ice and Cold Storage Company**; pioneered frozen goods imports; cafe at Fountain House.
- 1935
  - Carbonic acid gas plant installed; bulk ice cream produced in four-gallon buckets.
- 1941
  - Renamed to **Ceylon Cold Stores Limited**.
- 1950
  - Opened a new creamery and mineral water factory; production increased.
- 1966
  - Revamped cold storage; modern bottling line; re-introduced **Ice Palam** (popsicles).
- 1970
  - Listed on the Colombo Stock Exchange.
- 1991
  - Came under **John Keells Holdings Limited** after acquisition of the Whittalls Group.
- 1998
  - Modern bottling plant at Ranala soft drink plant.
- 2010
  - ** Elephant House** relaunched on 25 July; **KIK** cola launched in December.
- 2011
  - New bottle shape for Elephant House beverages.
- 2013
  - Head office relocated to **Vauxhall Street**, Colombo.
- 2016
  - Celebrated 150th anniversary.
- 2017
  - Incorporation of **The Colombo Ice Company (Pvt) Ltd**; foundation stone for Seethawaka factory.
- 2018
  - Machinery installed at Seethawaka facility (Avissawella CICL).

- Facilities and capabilities
  - Ranala Factory (since 1997): essence plant (ginger, vanilla), water/energy conservation, effluent treatment, battery-operated warehousing.
  - CICL Ice Cream Factory at Avissawella (commissioned 2018): largest Sri Lankan ice cream facility; 9.5-acre Seethawaka EPZ site; ISO/SLS certifications.

- Distribution
  - **164 distributors**; supply to **over 100,000 retailers** island-wide with real-time data sharing.
