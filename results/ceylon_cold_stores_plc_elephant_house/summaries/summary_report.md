## 4. Company History
- Early origins and branding
  - 1866 founding as the **Colombo Ice Company**
  - 1883 introduction of Aerated Water with the distinctive **“Elephant”** trademark
  - 1880 acquisition of J Maitland’s medical hall; growth milestones followed

- Corporate evolution
  - 1894 merger with Tom Walker’s company forming **New Colombo Ice Company Limited**
  - 1921 crown corks introduced for aerated water
  - 1925 cold storage facility for frozen products begins
  - 1932 acquisition of **Ceylon Creameries Limited** for reconstituted milk and ice cream
  - 1934 purchase of **Ceylon Ice and Cold Storage Company**; pioneering frozen imports
  - 1935 carbonic acid gas plant installed; bulk ice cream production expands
  - 1941 renamed to **Ceylon Cold Stores Limited**

- Modern era and expansion
  - 1950 new creamery and mineral water factory; production increases
  - 1966 revamped cold storage; new bottling line; re-introduced Ice Palam popsicles
  - 1970 listed on Colombo Stock Exchange
  - 1991 CCS comes under **John Keells Holdings Limited** after Whittalls Group acquisition
  - 1998 modern bottling plant at Ranala soft drinks plant
  - 2010 Elephant House relaunched; **KIK** cola launched
  - 2011 new bottle shape; 2013 head office relocation to Colombo
  - 2016 CCS marks 150th anniversary
  - 2017 incorporation of The Colombo Ice Company (Pvt) Ltd; Seethawaka factory planning
  - 2018 Seethawaka (Avissawella CICL) facility commissioned; Ranala plant continues operations

- Facilities and coverage
  - Ranala Factory since 1997 for beverages and ice cream
  - CICL Ice Cream Factory at Avissawella (largest in Sri Lanka) commissioned 2018
  - 164 distributors; real‑time data sharing with over 100,000 retailers island-wide
