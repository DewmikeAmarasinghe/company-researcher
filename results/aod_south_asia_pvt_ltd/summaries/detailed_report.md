## 2. Business Model and Revenue

- Northumbria University UK partnership model
  - Delivers UK-degree programs in Sri Lanka through AOD, with students enrolled as Northumbria University students.
  - Degree certificates identical to UK peers; eligible to graduate in the UK or Colombo.
  - 100% international faculty; moderation by Northumbria’s examination board; access to Northumbria international resources.
  - Strong industry links with built-in internships; graduate employment programme reported at 100% success for the last 7 years.
  - Recognized by the University Grants Commission Sri Lanka (UGC).

- Programs and delivery
  - Full-time BA (Hons) programmes across fashion, textiles, interior design, visual communication, motion graphics, animation, film/TV, 3D product design; foundations and diploma options; masters available.
  - E-learning options: three categories of online learning (online, offline, blended) with a university-backed learning portal and international faculty.

- Revenue streams
  - Tuition/degree fees aligned to Northumbria-based programs (local and international cohorts).
  - Application fees: LKRs 5,000 for Sri Lankan students; GBP 33 for international students.
  - Industry internships as part of the program adding contextual value (indirect revenue via partnerships and enhanced student outcomes).

- Pricing strategy and financial options
  - Flexible study financing: Study Now Pay Later; flexible monthly payment plans.
  - 0% interest 12-month payment plan for credit cards from selected banks.
  - Up to 20% savings on full payment options.

  - **De Montfort University (DMU)**: DMUxAOD partnership offers internationally accredited postgraduate design programs beginning in Sri Lanka; **MA Design Innovation** and **MA Design Management and Entrepreneurship**; Contour Fashion focus via DMU’s program.
