## 2. Business Model and Revenue
- Market position: **Sri Lanka’s largest digital health company** serving **200,000+ users**; operations expanded to **India, Maldives, Cambodia**; corporate footprint grows with ~**60+ corporates**
- Business model: **B2B**, **B2B2C**, and **B2G** offerings; corporate subscriptions provide unlimited on-demand consultations via the app; some employers supply tablets for non-smartphone users
- Revenue streams: corporate subscription fees for employee access; **TPA/insurance administration** services through insurer partnerships; government CSR and broader health-ecosystem services as expansion
- Pricing strategy: illustrative entry pricing cited as **Rs 35** or **INR 15** for employee access; pandemic-driven pivot from B2B2C toward direct-to-consumer **B2C** to capture rising demand
- Growth signals: government collaborations and large employer uptake underpin scale ambitions

## 3. Products and Services
- Telemedicine services
  - Channel a doctor in **three taps**
  - **SLMC-registered** doctors; GP or specialist
  - On-demand GP 24/7; call within scheduled time; prescription delivered via app
  - Steps: select doctor → choose date/time → send messages/reports → doctor calls → prescription delivered via app
- At-home and delivery services
  - `oLabs` at-home blood tests (order from app)
  - `oPharma` medicines delivered within two hours (order from app)
- Health Hub
  - Health articles and guides
  - Example: “A Comprehensive Guide to Understanding HIV and AIDS”; “Beginner’s Guide to Managing Finances”
- What we treat (care categories)
  - Minor Urgent Care; Chronic Care; Behavioral care
  - Women's and Men's health
- Endorsements and reach
  - Government endorsement as primary COVID-19 response
  - **200,000+** registered users; **1,150+** doctors; **110+** specialisations; **4.8/5** average rating; **70+** corporate clients; **210,000+** consultations
- Availability
  - Languages: **English**, Sinhala, Tamil
  - Focus: Sri Lanka market with SLMC-registered professionals

## 4. Company History
- 2017: Founded by **Heshan Fernando** and co-founders to make primary healthcare accessible online; raised **US$1M** from LOLC, Brandix, Ajit Gunawardene
- 2019–2020: connected over **800 doctors** across 50+ specialities; telemedicine plus medicine delivery and mobile lab services; introduced **oPharma** and **oLabs**
- 2020: Ministry of Health appointed oDoc as national telemedicine partner; CSR initiative launched for nationwide access; revenues grow ~**5x** in 2020
- Partnerships and expansion: **Allianz Lanka**, **John Keells Holdings (JKH)**, **Vidal Health** (TPA services)
- Vision: position as digital front door to healthcare and insurance; expansion across South Asia and beyond
