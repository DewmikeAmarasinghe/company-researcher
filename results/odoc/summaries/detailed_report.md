## 2. Business Model and Revenue

- Market position and scope
  - **Sri Lanka’s largest digital health company**; serves **200,000+ users**, **60+ corporates**; operations expanded to **India, Maldives, Cambodia**; partnered with the government for the Sri Lanka National Telemedicine Platform.

- Business model
  - **B2B**, **B2B2C**, and **B2G** offerings.
  - Corporate clients provide employees (and dependents) with unlimited on-demand consultations via the app; some employers supply tablets for non-smartphone users.
  - Tied into insurance via a **Third-Party Administration (TPA)** product to streamline insurer administration, claims, and fraud prevention (see next section).

- Revenue streams
  - Corporate subscription fees for employee access ( primary revenue stream).
  - TPA/insurance administration services through partnerships with insurers (outsourced enrollment, benefits management, cashless hospitalization, claims processing, fraud prevention).
  - Government/corporate CSR and broader health-ecosystem services as expansion of the health and insurance super app.

- Pricing strategy
  - Pricing example cited: **starting at around Rs 35** or **INR 15** for employee access (illustrative entry pricing reported in interviews).
  - Pivoted from B2B2C toward direct-to-consumer (B2C) during the pandemic to capture rising demand; enhanced marketing and user experience (website redesign) to grow adoption.

- Partnerships and growth
  - Partnerships with Dialog & Mobitel (carrier billing, eChannelling) and Sri Lanka Ministry of Health (CSR National Telemedicine Platform).
  - Funding history: **$1M pre-Series A** led by Techstars (with Hustle Fund, Unpopular Ventures); prior rounds include seed and GSMA grants.
  - Growth metrics: **1,000+ doctors**, **200,000 lives**, **65+ corporates**; revenues grew **5x in 2020** and **10x by mid-2021**.

## 4. Company History

- 2017
  - Founded by **Heshan Fernando** (CEO) and co-founders including Dr. Sohan Dharmaraja and Dr. Janaka Wickremesinghe; launched to make primary healthcare accessible online in Sri Lanka.
  - Raised **US$1 million** in funding from **LOLC**, **Brandix** and Ajit Gunawardene.

- Growth and reach (early milestones)
  - By 2019–2020, connected over **800 doctors across 50+ specialities**; later reports cite **nearly 1,000 registered doctors** and serving over **200,000 lives** across Sri Lanka, India, and the Maldives.
  - Offered telemedicine consultations plus **medicine delivery** and **mobile lab** services; introduced **oPharma** and **oLabs**.

- 2020: Covid-19 catalyst
  - The Ministry of Health, Sri Lanka appointed oDoc as the national telemedicine partner, enabling free consultations with government doctors.
  - Company grew about **5x in 2020**; post-pandemic base maintained elevated telemedicine usage.
  - Launched Sri Lankan National Telemedicine Platform as a CSR initiative to improve access nationwide.
  - Corporate uptake expanded (over **40 corporate clients**).

- Partnerships and expansion
  - **Allianz Lanka**: 1-year partnership to create value for customers.
  - **John Keells Holdings (JKH)**: partnership to provide healthcare access to over **3,000 employees**.
  - **Vidal Health**: launched Third-Party Administration (TPA) services for insurers, powered by the Vings system (manages 120+ million policyholders).

- Vision and positioning
  - Aims to be the digital front door to healthcare and insurance, making high-quality care universally accessible, affordable, and personal.
