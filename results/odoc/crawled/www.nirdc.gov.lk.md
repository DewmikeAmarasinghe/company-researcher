NIRDC and State Mortgage and Investment Bank Initiate Discussions to Explore New Linkages Between Sri LankaтАЩs Research and Financial Sectors
  

Marking a significant step toward strengthening financial sector involvement in Sri LankaтАЩs research and development (R&D) landscape, an initial round of discussions was held on the 6th between the National Initiative for Research and Development Commercialization (NIRDC) and the State Mortgage and Investment Bank (SMIB).
For the innovations developed by Sri Lankan researchers and innovators to move beyond laboratories and prototype stages and reach the market as successful commercial ventures, active participation from the financial sector is vital. These discussions emphasized the importance of narrowing the existing gap between the research and financial sectors, with the objective of improving access to financial support for local researchers and enhancing the overall efficiency of innovation commercialization. In this context, attention was also given to the facilities currently available at the State Mortgage and Investment Bank (SMIB) that could be leveraged to release funding for research projects selected on a quarterly basis by NIRDC.
The meeting was attended by Senior Manager (Credit) Ashila Tennakoon, Assistant General Manager (Credit) Nuwan Rajapaksa, Senior Manager (Credit) Gayan Wijesinghe, and Marketing Officer Vikum Perera representing SMIB. NIRDC was represented by Director General Dr. Muditha D. Senarath Yapa, Director (Investments) Indunil Gunathilaka, and other senior officials. Such collaborative efforts are expected to contribute toward building a robust, innovation-led economy in Sri Lanka.

## **A New Renaissance in Sri LankaтАЩs R &D Sector: NIRDC Allocates LKR 227 million for 09 Selected Projects in the First Quarter. **
  

Marking a transformative step in Sri LankaтАЩs Research and Development (R&D) landscape, the National Initiative for R&D Commercialization (NIRDC), established under the Presidential Secretariat, held a certificate awarding ceremony yesterday (22nd). Financial facilities totaling LKR 227 million were allocated to nine selected projects for the first quarter. The certificates were presented by Dr. Nandika Sanath Kumanayake, Secretary to the President, and Professor Gomika Udugamasooriya, Senior Advisor to the President on Science and Technology. 
For the first quarter of this program, 82 project proposals valued at LKR 2.2 billion were submitted. Following a stringent and transparent five-stage evaluation process, nine projects were selected. The evaluation focused on several key pillars: Technical and Scientific Merit, Administrative and Financial Feasibility, Market Potential, Legal Compliance. Notably, the technical and scientific assessments were conducted by an independent panel of 200 subject matter experts. 
The core function of the NIRDC is providing "Gap-filling funding." Historically, many local research ideas remained confined to laboratories due to a lack of capital required to convert them into commercial products. NIRDC addresses this by providing: Direct Grants: Up to LKR 10 million. Recoverable Facilities: Between LKR 10 million and LKR 50 million, repayable after a one-year grace period. These funds are disbursed through the Bank of Ceylon (BOC) in installments, contingent upon the achievement of specific Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for each project.
The selected projects span diverse sectors that directly influence the national economy, including Eco-friendly Agriculture, Food Technology, Information Technology & Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning, Tourism, Apparel, and Energy. This initiative is a critical leap toward achieving an innovative-driven economic surge. The NIRDC plans to further strengthen this framework to foster sustainable growth. 
The event was graced by several Cabinet Ministers, including, Nalinda Jayatissa (Health and Mass Media), Prof. Chrishantha Abeysena (Science and Technology), Sunil Handunnetti (Industry and Entrepreneurship Development), Anil Jayantha Fernando (Labor), Wasantha Samarasinghe (Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development). Other key figures included Dr. Nandalal Weerasinghe (Governor of the Central Bank), Dr. Hans Wijayasuriya (Senior Advisor on Digital Economy), and J.M. Wijebandara (Advisor on Law), along with Ministry Secretaries, University Vice-Chancellors, heads of research institutes, and leading academics.

A Meeting with the R&D Divisions of the Tri-Forces and Police at the Presidential Secretariat
  

Another meeting of the Research and Development (R&D) divisions of the Tri-Forces and the Sri Lanka Police, coordinated by the National Initiative for R&D Commercialization (NIRDC), was held on 23 September 2025 at the Presidential Secretariat.
This meeting was a follow-up to the R&D stakeholder session conducted by NIRDC on 1 April 2025. The main purpose of the discussion was to address the interest expressed by the Sri Lanka Police in acquiring two technologies developed by the Sri Lanka Army and Sri Lanka Air Force: the Multifunctional Colour Light System produced by the Army and the Fabricated Paper Plate Manufacturing Machine developed by the Air Force.
Through the use of these technologies, the Sri Lanka Police expects multiple benefits, including reducing traffic congestion, improving road safety, minimizing human involvement in high-risk activities, promoting sustainability, supporting the circular economy, and reducing waste.
Participants included Brigadier Ranjith Meegasthanna and other senior officers of the Army, Air Commodore M.C.M. Fernando and officers of the Air Force, and Commodore J.U. Gunaseela, Director General of the Centre for Defence Research and Development (CDRD), along with other officers. Representing the Police were DIG (Traffic & Road Safety) Mr. A.I. Hapugoda, DIG (Headquarters) Mrs. R.A.D. Kumari, DIG (Research and Information) Mrs. A.G.N.D. Senevirathna, and other police officials. Mr. G.M.R.D. Aponsu, Senior Additional Secretary to the President, represented the Presidential Secretariat. From NIRDC, Director General Dr. Muditha D. Senarath Yapa, Director (Investment) Mr. Indunil Gunatilaka, and other officials were present.
This marks another successful intervention by NIRDC to leverage technologies developed by the tri-forces and the police for broader social benefit in Sri Lanka.

A Memorandum of Understanding to establish a laboratory with international accreditation facilities with a $15 million investment
  

A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed this morning (15) at the Presidential Secretariat between STEMedical of the USA and the Sri Lanka Institute of Biotechnology (SLIBTEC) to establish a laboratory with internationally recognised accreditation.
  

The MoU for this $15 million investment project, conducted under the guidance of the National Initiative for Research and Development Commercialisation (NIRDC), was signed by Professor Hans Keirstead, founder of the American company STEMedical and on behalf of the Sri Lanka Institute of Biotechnology (SLIBTEC), by its Chairman, Professor Samitha Hettige and Chief Operating Officer, Amali Ranasinghe.
  

For a long time, there has been a need to enhance the capacity of laboratories with internationally recognised accreditation for testing purposes across many sectors in Sri Lanka, including health, agriculture, cosmetics and Ayurveda, as well as in the import and export fields.
  

Professor Gomika Udugamasooriya, Senior Presidential Advisor on Science and Technology, who attended the event, said that today is a special day for Sri Lanka and that accreditation is very important for a country. Prof. Udugamasooriya emphasised the significance of a laboratory project with such accreditation facilities in achieving the governmentтАЩs objective of doubling the countryтАЩs export revenue by the year 2030.
  

This laboratory is expected to function as a main lab, working in conjunction with existing laboratories across the island, to ensure the quality of goods and services in various fields.
  

Professor Hans Keirstead, founder of STEMedical, stated that he is pleased with the current leadership in Sri Lanka, which he said has a broad vision for the scientific sector and has been able to identify developmental needs such as enhancing laboratory capacity. He also said that this project would bring many benefits to the country, not only clinically but also economically. Mr Keirstead further mentioned that it would attract the attention of economic experts and generate revenue by bringing items, such as medicines, from other countries to Sri Lanka for accreditation.
  

Professor Hans Keirstead, the founder of STEMedical in the United States, is an internationally recognised stem cell expert. He is also a respected entrepreneur in the field of regenerative medicine in the USA. It is also noteworthy that Professor Hans Keirstead, who currently serves as the Chief Executive Officer of the Human Immunome Project, is among the top 100 scientists in the world, as listed by Discover Magazine.
  

The PresidentтАЩs Senior Additional Secretary, Russel Aponsu; the Director-General of the National Initiative for Research and Development Commercialisation (NIRDC), Dr. Muditha Senarath Yapa; and the Chief Executive Officer of STEMedical in the United States, Dr. Nistor Gabriel Loan, also attended the event.

Special Meeting with WIPO Award Winner Dr. Nadeesha Chandrasena
  

On July 16, an inspiring online discussion took place between Dr. Nadeesha Chandrasena, a recent award winner at the WIPO Awards Ceremony hosted by the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), and Professor Gomika Udugamasooriya, Senior Presidential Advisor on Science and Technology, and Dr. Muditha D. Senarath Yapa, Director General of the National Initiative for Research and Development Commercialization (NIRDC).
This cordial discussion had two main goals: to congratulate Dr. Chandrasena on her outstanding achievement and to explore how her "Smart Drain" innovation could be integrated into Sri Lanka's urban development projects and future growth initiatives.
During the conversation, they noted that modern urban planning, especially managing rainwater and wastewater, faces significant hurdles due to climate and environmental shifts. Dr. Nadeesha Chandrasena further explained that her "Smart Drain" system can effectively prevent flooding often caused by solid waste buildup in traditional drains.
The potential to incorporate this new invention into upcoming urban plans and projects, such as the "Clean Sri Lanka" initiative, was also thoroughly discussed.

Competitive Examination to Recruit Technically Qualified Patent Agents.
  

On July 9th, a productive meeting took place at the Presidential Secretariat between the National Initiative for Research and Development Commercialization (NIRDC) and various patent agent organizations. The primary objective was to explore the feasibility of conducting a competitive examination to appoint technically qualified patent agents, a crucial step that has been missing until now.
This step was taken following a request received by NIRDC as a research proposal. The discussion addressed a long-standing issue in the country's weak patent mechanism, which has been highlighted previously. Currently, Sri Lanka receives approximately 200 patent applications annually, but over half are rejected. A major contributing factor to this is the shortage of technically sound patent agents and the poor quality of applications prepared by unqualified or untrained agents. Despite existing legal provisions, a formal competitive exam to recruit technically skilled patent agents has never been held, worsening these systemic flaws.
To overcome these shortcomings, the participants at the meeting agreed to conduct a competitive examination for recruiting technically qualified patent agents within three months and to present an action plan for this purpose. This initiative aims to significantly improve the quality and reliability of patent filings and support the development of a strong innovation and intellectual property framework in the country.
Attendees included Professor Gomika Udugamasooriya, Senior Presidential Advisor on Science and Technology, Mr. G.M.R.D. Aponsu, Senior Additional Secretary to the President, and Mr. Peter M. Gunawardena, Additional Director General, representing the Presidential Secretariat. From NIRDC, Dr. Muditha D. Senarath Yapa, Director General, and other representatives were present. Also in attendance was Mr. Nalinda Athapattu, Assistant Director of the National Intellectual Property Office (NIPO), among other institutional representatives.
