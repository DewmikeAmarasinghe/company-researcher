## 2. Business Model and Revenue

- Core business model
  - **PickMe** is the official brand of **Digital Mobility Solutions Lanka PLC.**, a software service provider founded in **June 2015**.
  - Operates as an on‑demand mobility and logistics platform using **Big Data** to improve efficiency, reliability, and safety of mobility services.
  - Uses an **existing dependable fleet** to power its services, enabling scale across rides, deliveries, and other mobility needs.

- Platforms, products and initiatives
  - **Ride-hailing, food delivery, and parcel delivery** via the PickMe app (including PickMe Flash).
  - **PickMe Flash** parcel delivery:
    - Pricing model: entirely **distance-based**; no weight or waiting-time charges; no hidden charges.
    - Delivery options: **Flash**, **Flash L** (tuks), **Flash XL** (cars); same‑hour delivery; multi-drops; real-time tracking; 24x7 support.
    - Insurance up to a specified amount; supports bulk/business deliveries.
  - **PickMe for Business**:
    - All-in-one platform for company transportation, meal services, and local delivery.
    - Features: dispatch taxis, invoicing, account management; reduces administrative overhead; scalable for various industries.
    - Benefits highlighted: **Optimize Costs**, **Efficient Commutes**, **Flexible Services**, **Operational Excellence**.

- Revenue streams and scale indicators
  - Platform processes **over Rs. 100 billion in transactions** and has enabled **200 million+ journeys** covering over **900 million kilometers**.
  - Revenue/monetization channels include platform transactions for rides, deliveries, and meals; **PickMe Flash** pricing and bulk business deliveries; and corporate invoicing via **PickMe for Business**.

- Payments and incentives
  - Payment options: **card payments, PickMe points, QR payments, cash**.
  - Loyalty through rewards and tiered programs.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

- **Overview**
  - Digital Mobility Solutions Lanka PLC operates **PickMe**, Sri Lanka’s leading app-based mobility platform for ride-hailing and on-demand delivery; launched in **2015** by **Jiffry Zulfer** and backed by the **IFC**. *Sri Lanka’s largest ride-hailing and food-delivery platform.*

- **Scale**
  - **>100,000** active drivers
  - **20,000** joined in the last year
  - **201-500** employees (as of 2024)

- **Services and initiatives**
  - **Ride-hailing and on-demand delivery**; *PickMe Flash* for goods transport (gas cylinders, groceries)
  - Airport operations expanded (June 2023) to improve tourist access
  - Government passes for drivers during curfews; collaboration with private banks for cash delivery
  - Insurance programs extending to off-duty drivers and their families
  - *Nena Pahana*: annual incentive/earn-more program for drivers

- **Market dynamics and competition**
  - Closest rival: **Uber** (operates in five districts and about 40 cities)
  - Growth aided by pandemic welfare initiatives delivering essentials and hospital transport
  - Non-platform tuk-tuk drivers pushing back; some drivers consider quitting due to commission and competition
  - Commission structure: **12%** per ride (reduced to **7%** if daily earnings exceed **8,000 LKR**); fuel typically consumes ~40% of fare

- **Investments and partnerships**
  - IFC-backed funding (seed round in 2018)
  - API-driven payments collaboration with **Standard Chartered Bank** to enable instant payments, 24/7 collections, and improved driver onboarding and reconciliation

- **Growth and impact**
  - Used by over 100,000 Sri Lankans for supplementary income; played a pivotal role in delivering goods during crises and supporting tourism mobility.

## 23. Case Studies

- **Digital Mobility Solutions Lanka PLC (PickMe)** overview
  - Public company listed on Colombo Stock Exchange (ticker **PKME**); founded **2014**; CEO **Jiffry Zulfer Hassen**; Chairman **Ajith Gunewardene**; revenue **LKR 3,919 million (2024)**; net income **LKR 679 million (2024)**; employees **>300**.
  - Products: **PickMe Taxi Hailing App**, **PickMe Food**; services: taxi hailing and delivery.

- **Scale and impact**
  - Enabled **200 million+ journeys**; covered **900+ million kilometers**; processed over **Rs 100 billion** in transactions.
  - 2024 IPO: oversubscribed; offered **13.04%** of shares to raise ~**LKR 1.5 billion**; listing date **13 Sep 2024**.
  - Milestones: 2021–2023 expansion into logistics/food, 2022 PickMe Pass, 2023 Nepal entry; 10-year anniversary coverage noting substantial user/driver growth.

- **Key case studies and projects**
  - **Case study: 42Gears** — use of `SureMDM` and `SureLock` for device management and lockdown; centralized control and app-only usage improved driver efficiency and reduced downtime.
  - **API payments with Standard Chartered** — API-based connectivity enabling instant payments/collections, 24/7 fund access, real-time alerts, streamlined onboarding and fast reconciliation.
  - **University collaboration (University of Moratuwa, 2025)** — multi-modal mobility integration (buses, trains, three-wheelers); aims for scalable, locally relevant Sri Lankan solutions; GPS coverage and low-literacy UI considerations.
  - **YAMU acquisition (2019)** — expanded into food/hotel reviews; YAMU had significant monthly active users and pageviews prior to acquisition.
  - **Awards** — **e-Swabhimani 2016** for Inclusion & Empowerment; UN World Summit Awards commendation. 

- **Technology and growth initiatives**
  - Backend: **100+ microservices**; platforms: **Google Cloud Platform** and **Microsoft Azure**; deployment with **Docker/Kubernetes**; messaging via **Apache Kafka**; services coded in **Go**.
  - 2018 onward: launched **PickMe Logistics and Food Delivery**; **PickMe Flash** (2019); **PickMe Marketplace/Pass** (2021–2022). 
  - COVID-19: vaccination-status display, passenger incentives, separators; Flash delivered 8,200+ orders during lockdowns.
