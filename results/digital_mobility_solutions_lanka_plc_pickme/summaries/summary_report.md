## 3. Business Model and Revenue
- Core model
  - PickMe operates as an on‑demand mobility and logistics platform leveraging **Big Data** to enhance efficiency, reliability, and safety
  - Utilizes an **existing dependable fleet** to scale rides, deliveries, and other mobility needs
- Platforms and initiatives
  - Ride-hailing **Ride**, food delivery **Food**, and parcel delivery **PickMe Flash** via a single app
  - **PickMe for Business** provides an all‑in‑one platform for company transportation, meals, and local delivery
- Revenue streams
  - Platform transactions across rides deliveries meals
  - PickMe Flash distance-based pricing including bulk business deliveries
  - Corporate invoicing and account management through PickMe for Business
- Payments and loyalty
  - Payments accepted via **card**, **PickMe points**, **QR payments**, and **cash**; loyalty programs and tiers

## 4. Products and Services
- Core offerings
  - **Ride-hailing app**
  - **Food delivery** via PickMe Food
  - **Parcel delivery and logistics** via PickMe Flash
- PickMe Flash – on‑demand parcel delivery
  - Distance-based pricing with no weight or waiting-time charges
  - Same‑hour delivery with options: **Flash** (standard) **Flash L** (tuks) **Flash XL** (cars) **Flash XXL**
  - Multi-delivery capability: up to three locations per trip; real‑time tracking; in‑app feedback; 24x7 support
  - Insurance for packages up to a specified amount; availability on mobile and web
- Business solutions – PickMe for Business
  - Dispatch taxis, invoicing, and account management on an all‑in‑one platform
  - Mobility use cases: customer visits, factory/site inspections, courtesy rides, events
  - Benefits: optimize costs, efficient commutes, flexible services, operational excellence
- Platform accessibility
  - Local Sri Lankan platform with global standards; **QR code** download; available on **Google Play** and the **App Store**; supports mobile and web

## 5. Company History
- Corporate identity and location
  - **Digital Mobility Solutions Lanka PLC (PickMe)**; Colombo‑based Sri Lankan tech mobility company
  - Listed on Colombo Stock Exchange as **PKME.N0000**; ISIN **LK0495N00008**
- Founding timeline
  - **March 2014** founded by **Zulfer Hassen**; HQ in Colombo; serves Sri Lanka
  - **June 2015** launched as a passenger ride‑hailing app; backed by IFC
- Milestones
  - **Sept 2015** private funding of **Rs 150 million**
  - **June 2017** ~**6.5 million rides** completed; ~**35,000 rides/day**; ~**620k customers**
  - **July 2019** acquired **YAMU** (325k MAU; 615k monthly pageviews)
  - COVID‑19 era: driver vaccination status display passenger incentives contact tracing
  - **Sept 2024** IPO on CSE; offered **13.04%** of shares; aimed to raise **Rs 1.5 billion**; IPO oversubscribed on day one
- International expansion and scale
  - **2023** first international market in **Nepal**; ongoing scale as Sri Lanka’s leading mobility tech platform
- Key metrics snapshot (context)
  - Over the years, PickMe achieved **200 million+ journeys**, covered **900 million kilometers**, and processed transactions over **Rs 100 billion**

## 6. Competitive Landscape
- Landscape and positioning
  - Sri Lanka’s leading app‑based mobility platform for ride hailing and on‑demand delivery
  - Backed by **IFC** in early rounds; positioned as the largest and most established local player
- Scale and reach
  - **>100,000 active drivers**; growth in new driver sign‑ups; regional expansion
  - Employee base historically in the 201–500 range
- Services and initiatives
  - Ride‑hailing and on‑demand delivery; **PickMe Flash** for parcel logistics
  - Airport operations expanded in **June 2023**; government support during curfews; insurance programs for drivers
- Market dynamics
  - Closest rival: **Uber**, operating in limited districts and cities
  - Pandemic‑era welfare initiatives aided adoption; some drivers face competition and commission pressure
- Investments and partnerships
  - **IFC**‑backed funding (seed round in 2018) and API payments collaboration with **Standard Chartered Bank** enabling 24/7 collections and real‑time onboarding
- Growth and impact
  - Provided income opportunities for tens of thousands of Sri Lankans; played a critical role in crisis logistics

## 11. Technology Stack
- Core technology and infrastructure
  - Backend architecture: **microservices (>100 microservices)**; primary language **Go**
  - Containerization and orchestration: **Docker** and **Kubernetes**

- Platform reach
  - Platform supports mobile and web access; service stack supports ride hailing meals and parcel delivery across Sri Lanka

- Notable integration and product evolution
