## 4. Company History

Founded in 1997, 
hSenid Mobile Solutions (Pvt) Ltd is a prominent Sri Lankan multinational technology company that evolved from the broader hSenid Group to specialize in telecommunications, banking, and enterprise software.
Founding Story
  * **Origin (1997):** Founded by Dinesh Saparamadu under **hSenid Software International** with just three employees in a single room.
  * **Initial Focus:** The company began by providing high-quality software products globally, with its first major success in Human Resource (HR) applications before diversifying into the mobile and wireless sectors.


Growth and Global Expansion
  * **Diversification:** Over time, the company transitioned into a specialized provider for the telecom and financial markets, eventually forming hSenid Mobile Solutions as a dedicated unit for mobile/wireless software.
  * **Geographical Reach:** By 2026, the company operates across multiple continents with offices in the USA, Singapore, Australia, Kenya, India, and Bangladesh, while maintaining primary R&D centers in Colombo and Kandy, Sri Lanka.
  * **Scale:** The company now handles nearly **50 million transactions per day** and connects over **300 million subscribers** worldwide.

  * **Technological Changes** : The necessity to continuously adapt to rapid technological changes (e.g., in AI, cloud computing) presents a constant challenge and requires ongoing investment in R&D and talent.


Opportunities and Threats
Opportunities
  * **Rapid Digitization & AI Adoption**: There is an immense opportunity in the rapid adoption of cloud Human Capital Management (HCM) products and enterprise-grade AI solutions in the APAC and African regions, as well as domestically.

Threats
  * **Regulatory Changes** : The company operates in sectors (telecom, finance) that are subject to potential regulatory changes in domestic and foreign laws and taxes, which can impact operations and profitability.
  * **Global Market Competition** : Operating in the global market means competing with established international tech giants and other emerging tech hubs, which can be challenging despite the "affordable expertise" advantage of the Sri Lankan tech ecosystem.
  * **Cybersecurity & Data Privacy**: As a provider of telecom and financial software, the risk of security breaches and the need to maintain high levels of performance and security are paramount concerns.
  * **Infrastructure Limitations** : While internet usage is rising in Sri Lanka, connectivity and general infrastructure still need improvement in some areas, which could hinder the seamless deployment and adoption of some advanced digital solutions.
