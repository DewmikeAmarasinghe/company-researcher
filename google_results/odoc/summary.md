# Executive Summary: oDoc

## 1. Founders and Leadership
- Founders: **Heshan Fernando**, **Dr. Janaka Wickremasinghe**, **Dr. Sohan Dharmarajah**, **Inshard Naizer**, **Sanjay Jayawardena**.
- Current CEO: **Lahiru Karunarathna** (as of late 2024/early 2025).
- Chairman & Co-Founder: **Heshan Fernando**.
- Key leadership: **Jenon Fernando** (Head of Operations); **Anisha Yasaratne** (Chief Operating Officer); **Minura Ratnasekera** (Vice President of Growth).
- Note: Multiple sections reference slightly different naming (e.g., Heshanka/Heshan); core leadership remains centered on the founder and executive team above.

## 2. Business Model and Revenue
- Model: **telemedicine-led health ecosystem** with a hybrid **B2B and B2C** approach.
- Revenue split: approximately **50/50** between B2B (corporate) and B2C (direct consumers).
- Revenue streams:
  - **Corporate Subscriptions** (per-employee monthly fees for unlimited consultations).
  - **Direct Consultation Fees** (PAYG with commission by oDoc).
  - **Value-Added Services** (oPharma pharmacy and oLabs mobile lab services).
  - **TPA Services** (outsourced administration for insurers).
  - **Partnerships** (carrier billing and integration with mobile operators).
- Expands beyond telemedicine into **TPA**, medicine delivery (**oPharma**), and at-home lab testing (**oLabs**).

## 3. Products and Services
- Telemedicine
  - **oDoc for You** – one-off video/audio consultations with SLMC-registered doctors; GP access within 3 minutes.
  - **oDoc Family** – family subscriptions covering up to 4 members with unlimited doctor appointments.
  - **Specialist Consultations** – access to 1,000+ doctors across 60+ specializations.
  - **Mental Health Services** – behavioral care (depression, PTSD, anxiety, addiction counseling).
  - **Chronic Care Management** – remote monitoring and prescription refills.
- Diagnostic & Pharmacy
  - **oPharma** – online pharmacy with 2-hour delivery in Colombo and suburbs.
  - **oLabs** – mobile home-based lab collection within 2–3 hours (Colombo vicinity).
- Corporate & Public Offerings
  - **oDoc for Business** – corporate plans with unlimited employee/family access.
  - **National Telemedicine Platform** – government platform for free remote consultations (MOH partnership).
  - **COVID-19 Assessment Tool** – AI-driven, WHO-based self-assessment (free).
- Loyalty & Perks
  - **oDoc Loyalty Program** – 10–20% discounts at partner healthy restaurants/cafes.

## 4. Company History
- Founded: **2017** to make primary healthcare universally accessible in Sri Lanka.
- 2017: Official app launch; seed round of **$1M** (largest seed in Sri Lanka then).
- 2019: Expansion to **India** (Bangalore) and **GSMA Ecosystem Accelerator** grant of **$600k**.
- 2020: COVID-19 as catalyst; revenue growth ~5x; 474% increase in new user registrations.
- 2021: Pre-Series A funding of **$1M** led by **Techstars**, **Hustle Fund**, and **Unpopular Ventures** (valuation doubled vs prior).
- Growth milestones: 200k+ lives covered; 1,000+ partner doctors across 40+ specializations; expanded to Maldives and India.

## 5. Competitive Landscape
- Direct Competitors:
  - **Doc990** (Dialog Axiata & Asiri Hospital Holdings) – tele-doctor plus extensive doctor network.
  - **eChannelling** (SLT-Mobitel) – telemedicine, online prescriptions, home labs.
  - **MyDoctor.lk** – video/phone/email consultations; emergency home visits with ambulances.
  - **Udoc.lk** – 24/7 medical care and virtual wellness screenings.
  - **Deedmed** – online doctor bookings, follow-ups, medicine delivery.
  - **eDoc** – teleconsultation and appointment bookings.
- Specialized Alternatives:
  - **Ayubo Life**, **Healthnet**, **Lanka Doctor** (wellness, online pharmacy, knowledge base).
- Market positioning: oDoc positioned as Sri Lanka’s leading digital health platform with broad B2B/B2C reach.

## 6. Market Position
- Market Leader in Sri Lanka’s digital health/telemedicine segment.
- User base and partnerships: **200k–300k+ users** over time; **60+ leading Sri Lankan corporates**; government partnership for national telemedicine platform.
- Recognized as top competitor in Sri Lankan telemedicine according to Tracxn (Sept 2025 analysis), ranked 1st among active competitors.

  - Data security and potential malpractice risk; infrastructural gaps in remote areas.
- Opportunities:
  - Strong growth in **B2B/enterprise** and underserved low-income segments.
  - Vertical integration: growing demand for **oPharma** and **oLabs**.
