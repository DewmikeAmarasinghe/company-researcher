## 2. Business Model and Revenue

oDoc operates as a 
**healthcare super-app** in Sri Lanka, utilizing a hybrid business model that spans **B2B (Business-to-Business)** and **B2C (Business-to-Consumer)** sectors. Its strategy centers on making primary healthcare accessible and affordable through a digital-first ecosystem.
Business Model
oDoc's core model is a **telemedicine-led health ecosystem** that bridges online consultations with offline fulfillment.
  * **B2B Focus:** Originally, oDoc primarily targeted corporates, offering healthcare as an employee benefit. By 2026, it serves over **70 local corporates**.
  * **B2C Pivot:** During the pandemic, oDoc expanded into the B2C market to serve individuals directly. Revenue is now split approximately **50/50** between these two segments.
  * **Expansion:** Beyond consultations, the model integrates **Third-Party Administration (TPA)** for insurance companies, medicine delivery (**oPharma**), and at-home lab testing.


Revenue Streams
oDoc generates income through diverse channels across its healthcare and insurance verticals:
  * **Corporate Subscriptions:** Monthly per-employee fees paid by businesses to provide staff with unlimited medical consultations.
  * **Direct Consultation Fees:** A **pay-as-you-go (PAYG)** model where individual users pay a fee per session, with oDoc taking a percentage commission.
  * **Value-Added Services:** Revenue from its mobile pharmacy, **oPharma** , and mobile lab unit services.
  * **TPA Services:** Fees from insurance companies for outsourcing administration tasks like policyholder enrollment and claims processing.
  * **Partnerships:** Integration with mobile operators like **Dialog** and **Hutch** for subscription-based products and carrier billing.

## 3. Products and Services

oDoc is a digital health platform in Sri Lanka that integrates telemedicine, pharmaceutical delivery, and laboratory services into a single ecosystem
.
**1. Telemedicine Services**
  * **oDoc for You:** Provides one-off video or audio consultations with Sri Lanka Medical Council (SLMC) registered doctors. Users can connect with a General Practitioner (GP) within 3 minutes.
  * **oDoc Family:** Subscription packages for families (covering up to 4 members) that offer unlimited doctor appointments for a monthly fee.
  * **Specialist Consultations:** Access to over 1,000 doctors across 60+ specializations, including pediatrics, gynecology, dermatology, psychiatry, and sexual health.
  * **Mental Health Services:** Dedicated behavioral care for conditions such as depression, trauma (PTSD), anxiety, and addiction counseling.
  * **Chronic Care Management:** Remote monitoring and prescription refills for long-term conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and cholesterol.


**2. Diagnostic & Pharmacy Services**
  * **oPharma:** An online pharmacy service that delivers prescribed medications and health products directly to users' doorsteps. It guarantees 2-hour delivery in Colombo and its suburbs.
  * **oLabs:** A mobile laboratory service where professional teams collect blood or other samples from a patient's home within 2-3 hours of a request. This service is primarily available in Colombo and its suburbs.


**3. Corporate & Public Offerings**
  * **oDoc for Business:** Tailored healthcare plans for organizations starting at a low monthly cost per employee. It provides unlimited consultations for employees and their families to reduce absenteeism.
  * **National Telemedicine Platform:** In partnership with the Ministry of Health (MOH), oDoc hosts a platform for government clinic patients to receive free medical advice and digital prescriptions remotely.
  * **COVID-19 Assessment Tool:** A free, AI-driven self-assessment tool based on WHO guidelines, available in multiple languages.


**4. Loyalty & Perks**
  * **oDoc Loyalty Program:** Members receive exclusive discounts (typically 10-20%) at partnered healthy restaurants and cafes such as Bowl’d, Cafe Kumbuk, and Fitmeals.

## 4. Company History

oDoc is Sri Lanka’s leading digital health platform, providing video consultations, electronic prescriptions, and medicine delivery
. Founded in 2016, it has grown from a local startup into a regional player covering lives in Sri Lanka, India, and the Maldives.
Founding & Core Concept
  * **Founders:** The company was founded by **Heshan Fernando** (CEO), **Dr. Sohan Dharmaraja** , **Inshard Naizer** , and **Dr. Janaka Wickremasinghe**.
  * **Mission:** To make high-quality healthcare universally accessible, affordable, and personal.
  * **The Problem:** The founders spent 12 months building a "360-degree solution" to move healthcare beyond a mere "medical problem" and solve it as a **design and behavior challenge**.
  * **Initial Funding:** In 2017, oDoc raised **$1 million in seed funding** , which was then the largest seed round for a Sri Lankan startup. Investors included Ajit Gunewardene (John Keells), Phoenix Ventures (Brandix), and Loits (LOLC).


Growth & Regional Expansion
  * **2017:** Official launch of the oDoc App for public and corporate use.
  * **2019:** Expansion into the Indian market, specifically targeting **Bangalore** as a hub for its regional services.
  * **2019:** Received a **$600,000 grant** (Rs. 42 million) from the GSMA Ecosystem Accelerator Innovation Fund.
  * **2020:** The COVID-19 pandemic served as a major catalyst. Revenue grew **5X** , with a 474% increase in new user registrations.
  * **2021:** Closed a **$1 million Pre-Series A** funding round at double its previous valuation, led by Techstars along with Silicon Valley's Hustle Fund and Unpopular Ventures.


Key Milestones & Partnerships
  * **Corporate Healthcare:** Over **65+ major corporates** (including MAS, Brandix, and Daraz) provide oDoc as a health benefit to employees.
  * **National Platform:** Launched the **Sri Lankan National Telemedicine Platform** as a CSR project for the Ministry of Health during the pandemic.
  * **Strategic Services:** Launched **Third-Party Administration (TPA)** services in partnership with Vidal Health to help insurance companies manage claims and policyholder benefits.
  * **Current Scale:** As of recent reports, oDoc covers over **200,000 lives** with a network of more than **1,000 partner doctors** across 40+ specializations.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

In the Sri Lankan market, 
**oDoc** faces competition from several key digital health platforms that offer telemedicine, doctor channeling, and home-based medical services.
Direct Competitors & Alternatives
  * **Doc990 (Digital Health)** : A major competitor operated as a joint venture between **Dialog Axiata** and **Asiri Hospital Holdings**. It offers Tele Doctor consultations via audio/video, alongside a massive physical doctor channeling network with over **5,000 doctors** across **140 hospitals**.
  * **eChannelling** : A pioneer in the market, now part of the SLT-Mobitel group. While traditionally focused on hospital appointments, its app now includes **telemedicine** , online **prescription delivery** , and **home laboratory tests**.
  * **MyDoctor.lk** : One of the early movers in the local market, providing **video, phone, and email consultations**. It differentiates itself by offering additional services like **emergency doctor home visits** with ambulances and specialized support for travelers and pregnant women.
  * **Udoc.lk** : A newer entrant focused on providing **24/7 medical care** and virtual wellness screenings. It emphasizes accessibility for geographically isolated or economically vulnerable populations.
  * **Deedmed** : A digital healthcare provider with an online doctor booking app that facilitates **consultations, follow-up visits** , and instant medicine delivery.
  * **eDoc** : A Colombo-based platform providing **teleconsultation and appointment booking** services for various health conditions.


Specialized & Wellness Alternatives
  * **Ayubo Life** : While focusing heavily on **wellness and fitness tracking** , it also allows users to teleconsult with doctors and fitness professionals through its mobile app.
  * **Healthnet** : A specialized alternative for online pharmacy services, delivering pharmaceuticals directly to doorsteps.
  * **Lanka Doctor** : Acts as a comprehensive knowledge base and directory to help the public find specialists and schedule appointments.

## 18. Challenges and Opportunities

Analyzing the Sri Lankan market for 
**oDoc** , the business landscape is characterized by high growth potential offset by entrenched cultural and economic hurdles.
**1. Business Challenges**
  * **Cultural Adoption:** A primary obstacle is the deeply ingrained consumer belief that physical examinations (e.g., checking pulse or lungs) are essential for accurate diagnosis.
  * **Initial User Conversion:** Prior to the pandemic, convincing users to try their first online consultation was a major friction point.
  * **Skill Gaps:** Finding and retaining competent specialists and technical staff for digital implementation is a widespread issue for Sri Lankan firms.
  * **Digital Literacy & Trust:** Despite rising tech usage, there remains a lack of confidence in online medical systems and digital payment methods in certain segments.


**2. Risks**
  * **Macroeconomic Instability:** Fluctuations in the Sri Lankan rupee and high inflation rates create financial uncertainty for long-term investments.
  * **Regulatory Complexity:** Complex regulations from the **Central Bank of Sri Lanka** regarding fintech integrations and data protection pose compliance burdens.
  * **Data Security:** Like all telehealth platforms, oDoc faces risks related to patient privacy, potential malpractice claims, and data security breaches.
  * **Infrastructural Gaps:** Unreliable internet connectivity and technical support in remote areas hinder the platform’s accessibility and sustainability.


**3. Opportunities**
  * **Market Expansion:** Significant growth potential exists in the B2B space and low-income segments, which are currently underserved by major competitors.
  * **Vertical Integration:** There is a surge in demand for integrated services like home medication delivery (**oPharma**) and mobile laboratory testing, both of which saw triple-digit revenue growth recently.
  * **Regional Growth:** Using Sri Lanka as a launchpad, oDoc is expanding into **India** and looking toward other South and Southeast Asian markets where manufacturing hubs offer high demand for corporate healthcare.
  * **Corporate Healthcare:** Offering affordable subscription models to startups and SMEs allows oDoc to tap into businesses that cannot afford traditional medical insurance.


**4. Threats**
  * **Increasing Competition:** well-funded competitors are gaining traction, particularly in the consumer-facing (B2C) telemedicine space.
  * **Regulatory Shifts:** New regulations or increased trade barriers on medical technology could disrupt existing business models.
  * **Alternative Services:** Low-cost government gateways or other digital health substitutes could potentially divert users from premium platforms.
  * **Infrastructure Reliability:** A lack of national guidelines and legislation on telehealth specifically can lead to legal ambiguity.

## 22. Social Responsibility

**democratizing healthcare** through digital innovation and strategic community partnerships.
Core CSR and Sustainability Initiatives
  * **Accessible Healthcare for Corporates:** oDoc provides affordable healthcare solutions for employees, helping companies manage workforce wellness and reduce absenteeism through remote consultations.
  * **National Telemedicine Support:** During national crises, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, oDoc collaborated with the **Ministry of Health** and the **Information and Communication Technology Agency (ICTA)** to provide free or subsidized teleconsultations, reducing the burden on physical hospital infrastructure.

  * **Digital Prescription and Pharmacy Integration:** To promote environmental sustainability, oDoc utilizes digital prescriptions and integrates with local pharmacies for medicine delivery, minimizing paper waste and carbon footprints associated with travel.
  * **Health Literacy and Awareness:** The platform frequently hosts health education campaigns on social media and its app to promote preventive care and mental health awareness among Sri Lankans.


Strategic Partnerships
oDoc often partners with major Sri Lankan entities to expand its social impact:
  * **Hemas Holdings:** Collaboration to integrate healthcare services within broader corporate wellness programs.

## 24. Pricing Information

oDoc Sri Lanka offers several pricing tiers tailored for individual users, corporate employees, and specialized health memberships.
**Individual Subscription Plans**
These plans typically cover the primary subscriber and three additional family members with unlimited consultations.
  * **oDoc Pro:** A monthly subscription providing unlimited access to on-demand General Practitioners (GPs) and specialists.
  * **oDoc Mind:** Monthly plan specifically for mental health services, priced around **LKR 4,999** (sometimes discounted to **LKR 3,749**).
  * **oDoc Health Club Membership:** Annual membership typically priced at **LKR 8,000** , with promotional rates as low as **LKR 6,000**.
  * **oDoc Premier:** Annual high-tier membership costing **LKR 9,500** (discounted to **LKR 7,125**).


**Corporate & Business Packages**
Organizations can provide oDoc as a health benefit for their employees at a fixed monthly rate per head.
  * **Standard Corporate Rate:** Usually starts at **LKR 100 per employee per month**.
  * **Bulk/Factory Rates:** Large organizations may access rates as low as **LKR 25 to LKR 60 per employee per month** , covering the employee and three family members.


**Consultation & Delivery Fees**
For users without an active unlimited subscription, the following costs apply:
  * **Video/Audio Consultations:** Fees are set by individual doctors, starting from **LKR 300**.
  * **Medicine Delivery:**
    * **Within Colombo 1-15:** **LKR 250** for 2-hour express delivery.
    * **Outside Colombo 1-15:** **LKR 450** for express delivery.
  * **Government Doctor Consultations:** Available for **free** through a partnership with the Ministry of Health.


**Partner-Specific Free Access**
Several companies offer **free unlimited oDoc access** as a value-added service to their customers or employees:
  * **Insurance & Banking:** Partners like AIA Insurance, HNB, and Seylan Bank often provide free subscriptions to policyholders or staff.
  * **Telecommunications:** Hutch users can access special daily or monthly micro-plans for as low as **LKR 99**.
