## 2. What is the business model, revenue streams, pricing strategy of AOD South Asia (Pvt) Ltd. Sri Lanka?

- **Business Model**: AOD operates as a private higher education institute specializing in design disciplines. It partners with international universities (e.g., Northumbria University, UK) to offer accredited degrees internally in Sri Lanka. The model integrates education with industry initiatives like Sri Lanka Design Festival (SLDF) and Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week Sri Lanka (MBFWSL), fostering a creative ecosystem. Revenue is tuition-driven, with emphasis on employability and innovation. 

- **Revenue Streams**:
  - Primary: Tuition fees from undergraduate programs (e.g., BA Hons in Fashion, Interior, Graphic Design).
  - Secondary: Events (e.g., SLDF tickets: International Delegate Pass ~LKR 481,275), partnerships, and alumni success programs.
  - Estimated annual revenue: ~$36 million (2025 projection, though this may include group companies).

- **Pricing Strategy**: Competitive for South Asia, focusing on value through international accreditation. Foundation programs start post-O/Ls; full degrees are premium-priced due to UK partnerships. Specific fees not publicly listed, but summer camps cost LKR 25,000. Strategy emphasizes affordability for regional students while maintaining quality.

## 4. Detailed history, founding story, growth timeline, milestones of AOD South Asia (Pvt) Ltd. Sri Lanka

- **Founding Story**: Founded in 2000 by Linda Speldewinde at age 23 to address the lack of design education in Sri Lanka. Started as the first design school, bridging local heritage with international standards. 

- **Growth Timeline & Milestones**:
  - **2000**: Established in Colombo.
  - **2005-2010**: Expanded programs; partnered with Northumbria University for internal UK degrees.
  - **2015**: Led "Design for Sri Lanka" initiative; introduced more specializations.
  - **2016**: Launched fashion business programs for apparel industry.
  - **2018**: "Innovation Island" campaign to position Sri Lanka as creative hub.
  - **2019**: Colombo Innovation Tower launch; CEO Robert Meeder appointed.
  - **2020-2023**: Focus on sustainability; alumni successes at global fashion weeks.
  - **2024-2026**: SLDF 15th edition with India partnership; MoU with Chaudhary Group Nepal for craft exports; graduate exhibitions and awards.  

- **Growth**: From a small school to South Asia's pioneering design educator, with 100-415 employees and regional ambitions.
